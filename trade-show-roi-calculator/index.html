How to calculate trade show ROI

Add up the full cost — booth space, materials, travel, lodging, shipping, staff time and giveaways — then project revenue from the leads you collect: leads × close rate × average sale. ROI is (revenue − total cost) ÷ total cost × 100. Cost per lead is total cost divided by leads collected.

Don't forget the hidden costs

Staff time is the most-missed line item. If three people work a two-day show, that's real payroll and opportunity cost. Include it, or your ROI will look better than reality. On the upside, events often produce longer-term referrals that a single-campaign ROI won't capture.

How to improve event ROI

Pick shows where your actual buyers gather (home & garden shows for homeowners, lunch-and-learns for B2B referrers), capture leads digitally with a fast follow-up sequence, and offer a reason to act on the spot — a free assessment or a show-only offer. Speed of follow-up after the event is the biggest driver of close rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is a good trade show ROI?
It varies, but aim to at least cover cost several times over once leads close. Include staff time so the number is honest, and remember events also seed referrals.
How do I measure event leads?
Capture contacts digitally at the booth, tag them by event, and track how many book and close so you can attribute revenue.
Which events are worth it?
Ones where your buyers actually are. For home services, consumer home & garden shows and expos usually beat generic business events.
