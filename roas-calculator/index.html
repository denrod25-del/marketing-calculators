What is ROAS?

ROAS (return on ad spend) is the revenue you earn for every dollar of advertising: ROAS = revenue ÷ ad spend, shown as a ratio like 4:1. It's the go-to metric for evaluating paid campaigns. A 4:1 ROAS means $4 back for every $1 spent — the same as a 300% ROI.

ROAS vs ROI vs break-even

ROAS is gross revenue per ad dollar; ROI subtracts cost to show profit as a percentage. Neither tells you if you're actually profitable without margin. Your break-even ROAS = 1 ÷ gross margin — at a 50% margin you need 2:1 just to break even, so a "good" ROAS is always relative to your margin.

What is a good ROAS?

A common target is 4:1, but it depends on margin. At a 25% margin you need 4:1 just to break even; at 70% margin, 2:1 is already profitable. Add your gross margin above to see your profit-based ROAS and the break-even line you must clear.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate ROAS?
Divide revenue by ad spend. $16,000 revenue on $4,000 spend is a 4:1 ROAS.
What is a good ROAS?
Often cited as 4:1, but it depends on margin. Compare ROAS to your break-even ROAS (1 divided by gross margin).
What's the difference between ROAS and ROI?
ROAS is revenue per ad dollar as a ratio; ROI subtracts cost and shows profit as a percentage.
