How to calculate sales commission

The basic formula is commission = sales × commission rate. Many plans add a base salary plus an accelerator — a higher rate on sales above quota — to reward over-performance. This tool applies the standard rate up to quota and the accelerated rate on everything beyond it.

Common commission structures

Straight commission pays a flat percentage of sales. Base-plus-commission adds a salary for stability. Tiered or accelerated plans raise the rate as reps blow past quota, aligning pay with growth. Draw-against-commission advances pay that's later reconciled against earned commission.

Setting a fair rate

Set the rate against your gross margin so commissions stay affordable, and make sure top performers can earn meaningfully more. Clear, motivating plans with an above-quota accelerator drive the behavior you want — more closed revenue — without eroding profitability.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate sales commission?
Multiply total sales by the commission rate. Add a base salary and apply an accelerated rate above quota if your plan includes them.
What is a typical commission rate?
It varies widely by industry and margin, commonly in the single digits to low double digits of sales, often higher above quota.
What is an accelerator?
A higher commission rate applied to sales above quota, used to reward reps for exceeding their target.
