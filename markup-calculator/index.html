Markup vs margin

Markup and margin describe the same profit from different angles. Markup is profit as a percentage of cost; margin is profit as a percentage of price. A $50 item sold for $100 carries a 100% markup but a 50% margin. Confusing the two is one of the most common pricing mistakes.

Setting a price from markup

Selling price = cost × (1 + markup%). Choose a markup that covers not just product cost but your overhead and target profit. Because margin is always lower than markup, always check the margin your markup produces before committing to a price.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate selling price from markup?
Multiply cost by (1 + markup percentage). A $50 cost with 100% markup sells for $100.
What's the difference between markup and margin?
Markup is profit as a percent of cost; margin is profit as a percent of price. Markup is always the larger number.
How do I find markup from a price?
Subtract cost from price, divide by cost, and multiply by 100. Enter a selling price above and the tool solves it for you.
