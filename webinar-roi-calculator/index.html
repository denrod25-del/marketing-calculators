How webinar ROI works

A webinar funnel is: registrants → attendees → customers. Not everyone who registers shows up (typical attendance is 35%–50%), and a share of attendees convert. Total cost includes promotion, the platform, and the time to produce and present. Even the no-shows are valuable — they're leads you can follow up with the recording.

Boost attendance and conversion

Send reminder emails and texts before the event, keep it genuinely educational (not a pitch), and make a clear, time-limited offer at the end. Follow up fast with both attendees and no-shows — the replay plus a personal follow-up often converts as well as the live event.

Great for trust-based selling

Webinars and lunch-and-learns work best when the buyer needs education before deciding — B2B referral partners, higher-ticket services, or complex decisions. Teaching builds trust, and trust closes deals.

Frequently asked questions

What is a good webinar attendance rate?
Typically 35%–50% of registrants attend live. Reminders and a compelling topic push it higher; the rest are still follow-up leads.
How do I calculate webinar ROI?
Registrants times attendance rate gives attendees, times conversion gives customers, times average sale gives revenue; compare to total cost.
Are webinars worth it?
Yes for education-driven or higher-ticket sales — teaching builds the trust that closes deals, and no-shows remain valuable leads.
