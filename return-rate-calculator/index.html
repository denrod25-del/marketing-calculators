What is return rate?

Return rate is the share of orders that get sent back: returns ÷ orders × 100. It's a key health metric for any product business — high returns erode margin through refunds, return shipping, restocking and lost inventory value, and often signal a product, sizing or expectation problem.

Typical return rates

Rates vary widely by channel and category. Brick-and-mortar retail often sees single-digit returns, while online retail commonly runs higher — and apparel and footwear can be much higher still because of fit. Compare to your category norm and track the trend rather than chasing a universal target.

Reducing returns

Set accurate expectations with detailed descriptions, sizing guides, photos and reviews; improve quality control; and analyze return reasons to fix root causes. Lowering returns protects margin directly — a percentage point off your return rate can rival the impact of a pricing change.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate return rate?
Divide the number of returns by total orders and multiply by 100. 100 returns on 2,000 orders is a 5% return rate.
What is a good return rate?
It depends on category — retail is often single digits while online apparel runs much higher. Compare within your sector and watch the trend.
How do returns affect profit?
Each return costs refund value plus shipping, restocking and potential lost inventory, so returns eat directly into margin.
