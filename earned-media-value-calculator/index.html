What is earned media value?

Earned Media Value (EMV) estimates what your free exposure — press coverage, influencer mentions, organic social, shares — would have cost as paid advertising. The base is simply impressions ÷ 1,000 × a benchmark CPM, then a multiplier credits engagement, which is worth more than a passive impression because it carries trust.

How to use EMV

EMV is best for comparing PR, influencer and organic efforts on a common dollar scale and reporting the value of activity that has no direct invoice. Pick a realistic CPM for the channel and a conservative multiplier — inflated assumptions make EMV meaningless.

EMV's limits

EMV is an estimate, not real revenue. An earned impression isn't identical to a paid one, and engagement quality varies. Use it as a directional value and a comparison tool, and pair it with hard metrics like referral traffic, leads and sales for the full picture.

Frequently asked questions

How is earned media value calculated?
Impressions divided by 1,000 times a benchmark CPM gives the base ad-equivalent value; an engagement multiplier then credits interactions.
What multiplier should I use?
A conservative one — often 1x to 2x. Higher multipliers inflate EMV and reduce its credibility.
Is EMV real revenue?
No. It's an estimate of ad-equivalent value for comparison and reporting, not money earned. Pair it with real metrics like leads and sales.
