How much do Facebook ads cost in 2026?

Facebook (Meta) ads are bought on CPM — cost per 1,000 impressions — which averages about $11.62, typically ranging $7–$14 by industry. On a click basis the average CPC is roughly $0.60–$1.00, far below Google Search. Your real cost per lead depends on click-through and landing-page conversion.

How to lower your Facebook cost per lead

Strong creative is the biggest lever — video and native-looking images beat stock. Tighten targeting to your service area, use lead-form ads to cut friction, retarget site visitors (cheap and high-converting), and let the campaign exit the learning phase before judging results.

Frequently asked questions

How much should I spend on Facebook ads?
Enough to exit the learning phase — often $30–$50/day per ad set to start. Use the calculator to size budget against your lead goal and average sale.
What is a good CPM on Facebook?
$7–$14 is typical; tightly targeted or high-competition audiences run higher. Compare within your industry and objective.
Are Facebook or Google ads cheaper?
Facebook usually has a lower CPC and CPM, but Google Search captures higher-intent demand. Many local businesses run both.
