Why email has the highest ROI

Email consistently posts the best return of any marketing channel — often cited around $36–$42 in revenue per $1 spent — because the audience already opted in and the cost per send is tiny. The math: opens = list × open rate, clicks = opens × CTR, sales = clicks × conversion, revenue = sales × average order value.

How to raise email ROI

Grow and clean your list, segment so messages are relevant, write subject lines that earn the open, and add automated flows (welcome, cart abandonment, win-back) that convert far better than one-off blasts. Deliverability matters too — a healthy list protects your open rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is a good email marketing ROI?
Averages land around 36:1 to 42:1 (revenue to cost). Automated, well-segmented programs beat that; unsegmented blasts fall short.
How is email revenue calculated?
List × open rate × click rate × conversion × average order value, multiplied by sends per month.
What is revenue per email?
Total revenue divided by total emails sent (list × sends). It's a clean way to compare campaigns of different sizes.
