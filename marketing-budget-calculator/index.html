How much should you spend on marketing?

The standard rule is a percentage of gross revenue. Established businesses defending their position often spend 5%–10%; companies pushing for growth or in competitive markets spend 10%–20%. New businesses building awareness sometimes go higher for a period. The right number depends on your margins, growth goals and competition.

Allocating the budget

Once you have a total, split it across channels by expected return. Anchor spend on the proven, high-intent channels first (search, local, referrals), reserve a slice for testing new channels, and keep enough for the fundamentals — website, SEO and reviews — that make every other channel work harder.

Frequently asked questions

What percent of revenue should go to marketing?
Commonly 5%–10% to maintain and 10%–20% to grow. Local service businesses often land around 7%–12%.
Should the budget be based on revenue or profit?
Most use gross revenue for the top-line target, then sanity-check it against margins so marketing stays affordable.
How do I split the budget by channel?
Fund proven high-intent channels first, keep a test budget for new ones, and protect spend on website, SEO and reviews.
