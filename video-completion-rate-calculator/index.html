What is video completion rate?

Video completion rate, or view-through rate (VTR), is the share of video ad impressions that are watched to the end: completed views ÷ impressions × 100. It measures how well your creative holds attention across its full length — a core quality signal for video advertising.

What drives completion

Length, hook strength and relevance drive VTR. Shorter ads complete at higher rates; a compelling first few seconds keeps viewers from skipping; and well-targeted, relevant creative earns more full views. Skippable formats naturally show lower completion than non-skippable, so compare like with like.

Using VTR

Track VTR alongside completion cost (cost per completed view) to judge both engagement and efficiency. A high VTR with a reasonable cost per completed view means your creative and targeting are working. Test different lengths and openings, and cut what viewers abandon early.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate video completion rate?
Divide completed views by impressions (video starts) and multiply by 100.
What is a good VTR?
It depends on format and length — non-skippable and short ads complete higher. Compare within the same format and against your history.
What is cost per completed view?
Ad spend divided by the number of completed views — a measure of how efficiently you're buying full watches.
