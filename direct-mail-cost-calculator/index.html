How much does direct mail cost?

All-in direct mail runs roughly $0.30–$1.20 per piece in 2026 for common postcard formats, and up to $2–$3 for larger or premium mailers. Total cost is simply pieces × cost per piece. Postage is set nationally by USPS, so your city doesn't change the price — format and volume do.

Response rates and ROI

Direct mail response rates typically run 0.5%–2% — higher for targeted house lists, lower for cold saturation mail. Responses × close rate = customers, and customers × average sale = revenue. Because response volume is modest, direct mail works best when your average sale is high enough to cover the per-piece cost several times over.

Frequently asked questions

How much does it cost to mail 5,000 postcards?
At a typical $0.70 all-in per piece, about $3,500. EDDM saturation mail can drop it toward $2,250; premium formats push it higher.
What is a good direct mail response rate?
0.5%–1% is common for prospecting lists; 2%+ is strong and usually comes from targeted or repeat audiences.
Is EDDM cheaper than targeted mail?
Yes — Every Door Direct Mail skips list rental and qualifies for the lowest postage tier, so it's the cheapest per piece, but it's less targeted.
