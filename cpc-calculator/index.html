What is cost per click?

CPC is the price you pay for each click on a paid ad: CPC = ad spend ÷ clicks. It's the core unit of pay-per-click advertising on Google, Bing, Meta and most other platforms. A lower CPC stretches your budget further, but only matters alongside how well those clicks convert.

CPC vs cost per conversion

Cheap clicks that don't convert waste money. The number that actually drives profit is cost per conversion (spend ÷ conversions). Add your conversion rate above and the calculator shows what each customer or lead really costs — the figure to optimize against your margins.

What affects CPC

CPC is driven by competition, keyword or audience value, ad relevance and quality score, and bidding strategy. High-intent commercial keywords cost more; better creative and landing pages lower effective CPC by improving quality. Track CPC alongside conversion rate so you don't chase cheap clicks that never buy.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate cost per click?
Divide total ad spend by the number of clicks. $1,000 spent for 500 clicks is a $2.00 CPC.
What is a good CPC?
It varies by industry and platform — from a few cents to tens of dollars for competitive commercial keywords. A good CPC is one your conversion economics can support.
How do I lower my CPC?
Improve ad relevance and quality score, tighten targeting, test creative, and refine landing pages so more clicks convert.
