How much does print advertising cost?

Print rates span a huge range. A quarter-page in a local newspaper runs about $200–$500 and a full page around $2,000; regional dailies charge $5,000–$20,000 for a full page. Local magazines run about $1,100–$2,000 a full page, while national magazines reach $250,000–$700,000. Rates depend on circulation, size, color and frequency.

Making print pay off

Print works locally when the publication reaches your customers and you commit to frequency — one ad rarely lands, but repeated placement builds recognition. Include a clear offer and a trackable code or number, and negotiate: printed rate cards are usually a starting point, not the final price.

Frequently asked questions

How much does a newspaper ad cost?
About $200–$500 for a quarter page in a local paper and ~$2,000 for a full page; regional dailies and nationals cost far more.
How much does a magazine ad cost?
Local magazines run ~$1,100–$2,000 a full page; national magazines can reach $250,000–$700,000 per insertion.
Is print advertising still effective?
It can be for local, targeted publications with frequency and a trackable offer. Negotiate rates and measure with a dedicated code or number.
