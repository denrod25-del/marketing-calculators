What is repeat purchase rate?

Repeat purchase rate (RPR) is the share of customers who buy more than once: repeat customers ÷ total customers × 100. It's a direct read on loyalty and product/service satisfaction — and repeat buyers are far cheaper to sell to than new ones, so RPR is a quiet driver of profit.

Why repeat buyers matter

Returning customers spend more, cost less to reach, and refer others. Lifting RPR raises lifetime value and lowers your effective acquisition cost across the whole business. For many companies, a small increase in repeat rate beats a large increase in new-customer volume.

How to increase repeat purchases

Follow up after the first purchase, remind customers when it's time to buy again (maintenance, refills, renewals), run a simple loyalty or referral program, and deliver an experience worth returning for. Email and SMS to existing customers are among the highest-ROI ways to drive repeats.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate repeat purchase rate?
Divide the number of customers who purchased more than once by total customers, then multiply by 100.
What is a good repeat purchase rate?
It varies by industry; higher is better. For many businesses 20%–30%+ is solid, though subscription and consumable categories run higher.
How do I increase repeat purchases?
Follow up after purchase, send timely reminders, run loyalty or referral programs, and use email and SMS to existing customers.
