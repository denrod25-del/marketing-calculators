Why affiliate marketing is low-risk

With affiliate marketing you pay a commission only when a partner actually drives a sale, so your cost scales with revenue — there's no upfront ad spend to lose. Effective cost per sale is essentially your commission rate plus any platform fee, which makes the ROI easy to control.

Setting the commission rate

Commissions typically run 5%–30% of order value depending on margin and industry. Set it high enough to motivate partners but below your gross margin so each sale still profits. Services and digital goods (high margin) can afford richer commissions than thin-margin physical products.

Making it work

Recruit partners whose audience matches your customer, give them tracking links and creative, pay reliably, and watch for low-quality or fraudulent sales. Referral and affiliate programs share the same strength: you only pay for results.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate affiliate marketing ROI?
Revenue is affiliate sales times average order value. Subtract commissions and fees from gross profit to get net, then divide net by total program cost.
What is a typical affiliate commission?
Usually 5%–30% of order value, set below your gross margin so each sale remains profitable.
Is affiliate marketing worth it?
It's low-risk because you pay only on sales. It works best with partners whose audience matches your customer and clean sale tracking.
