How much does a billboard cost in 2026?

A standard static billboard runs roughly $1,200–$9,000 per month in most U.S. markets, with small towns and rural highways at the low end and major metros at the top. Digital (LED) billboards typically cost 30–50% more than a comparable static board. Premium placements like Times Square are in a different league entirely — often $15,000–$50,000+ per month.

What drives billboard pricing

Market size and traffic volume are the biggest factors, followed by format (digital vs static), board size, location quality (highway vs side street), and campaign length — longer commitments usually lower the monthly rate. Production and installation add a one-time cost for static boards (printing ~$1.50–$3.00 per square foot, install ~$200–$1,000).

Billboard CPM explained

Frequently asked questions

How much does a billboard cost per month?
Most static billboards cost $1,200–$9,000 per month depending on market; digital costs 30–50% more. Times Square and other premium spots run far higher.
Are digital billboards worth the premium?
Digital lets you rotate multiple creatives and change messaging instantly, and skips printing costs. It commands a 30–50% premium, so it pays off when you value flexibility or run multiple ads.
How is billboard cost calculated?
Monthly board rate × number of boards × campaign months, plus one-time production for static. CPM = total cost ÷ (total impressions ÷ 1,000).
