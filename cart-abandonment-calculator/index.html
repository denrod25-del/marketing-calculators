What is cart abandonment rate?

Cart abandonment rate is the share of shoppers who add to cart but don't buy: (carts − purchases) ÷ carts × 100. Across e-commerce it averages around 70%, so most of your ready-to-buy demand slips away at checkout — which is exactly why recovery is so valuable.

Recovering abandoned carts

A simple abandoned-cart email or SMS sequence typically recovers 5%–15% of abandoned carts — revenue you already earned the demand for. The calculator estimates recoverable revenue from your abandonment volume, average order value and a realistic recovery rate.

How to reduce abandonment

Cut checkout friction: fewer form fields, guest checkout, clear total costs upfront (shipping surprises are a top cause), trust signals, and multiple payment options. Then layer a recovery flow on top to win back the shoppers who still slip away.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate cart abandonment rate?
Subtract completed purchases from carts created, divide by carts created, and multiply by 100.
What is the average cart abandonment rate?
Around 70% across e-commerce, though it varies by industry, device and checkout experience.
How much revenue can I recover?
A recovery email or SMS flow typically wins back 5%–15% of abandoned carts. This tool estimates it from your volume and average order value.
