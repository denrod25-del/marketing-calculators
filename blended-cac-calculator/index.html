What is blended CAC?

Blended CAC divides all sales and marketing spend by all new customers — including those who came in organically, by referral or by word of mouth. It's your true, honest, all-in cost to acquire a customer, and it can't be gamed by attribution the way channel-level numbers can.

Blended vs paid CAC

Paid CAC (ad spend ÷ customers from ads) tells you how efficient your advertising is. Blended CAC tells you what the whole business actually pays per customer. Paid CAC is usually higher than blended, because organic customers pull the blended average down. Watch both: if blended CAC creeps toward paid CAC, your organic engine is weakening.

Using CAC well

Compare CAC to lifetime value — a healthy LTV:CAC ratio is around 3:1 or better. Track blended CAC over time as you scale spend; rising blended CAC signals diminishing returns, while stable CAC with growing volume means efficient growth.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate blended CAC?
Divide total sales and marketing spend by the total number of new customers from all sources during the period.
What's the difference between blended and paid CAC?
Blended CAC includes all customers and all spend; paid CAC counts only ad spend and customers from ads. Paid CAC is usually higher.
What is a good CAC?
One where lifetime value is comfortably higher — an LTV:CAC ratio around 3:1 or better is a common healthy target.
