How to allocate a marketing budget

Start from your total budget and assign a percentage to each channel based on where you get the best return and what stage your funnel needs most. This calculator turns those percentages into dollar figures and checks that they add up to 100% so nothing is over- or under-committed.

A common starting split

Many small and mid-sized businesses land near 40–50% paid acquisition (search + social), 20–30% owned/organic (SEO, content, email), and a remainder for brand, testing and other channels. Weight toward whatever is proven for your business, and reserve 10–15% to test new channels.

Revisit the mix regularly

Allocation isn't set-and-forget. Review channel ROI monthly and shift dollars toward what's working. Pair this with a budget-as-percentage-of-revenue calculation to size the total, then use this tool to divide it up.

Frequently asked questions

How should I split my marketing budget?
Weight channels by proven ROI and funnel need. A common starting point is roughly half to paid acquisition, a quarter to organic/owned, and the rest to brand and testing.
What if my percentages don't add up to 100%?
The calculator flags whether you're over or under 100% so you can rebalance before committing spend.
How often should I rebalance?
Review channel performance monthly and move budget toward what's working; keep 10–15% for testing new channels.
