What is sales velocity?

Sales velocity measures how much revenue your pipeline generates each day: (opportunities × win rate × average deal size) ÷ sales cycle length. It rolls the four levers of sales performance into a single number and tells you how fast money is actually moving through your funnel.

The four levers

You can raise velocity four ways: more opportunities, bigger deal size, a higher win rate, or a shorter sales cycle. Because cycle length is a divisor, shortening it can boost velocity as much as adding deals — often the most overlooked lever.

Using it to plan

Multiply daily velocity by the days in a period to forecast revenue, and model changes before you make them: what does a 5-point win-rate improvement or a 10-day faster cycle do to the number? It turns vague "work the pipeline" goals into concrete targets.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate sales velocity?
Multiply the number of opportunities by win rate and average deal size, then divide by the sales cycle length in days.
Why does a shorter sales cycle increase velocity?
Cycle length is the divisor — closing the same deals faster means more revenue flows through per day.
How do I improve sales velocity?
Increase opportunities, raise average deal size, improve win rate, or shorten the sales cycle. Any of the four lifts velocity.
