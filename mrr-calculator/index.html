What is MRR?

Monthly Recurring Revenue is the predictable subscription revenue you earn each month. The movement that matters is net new MRR = new + expansion − contraction − churn. Positive net new MRR means the business is growing under its own weight; negative means churn and downgrades are outrunning new sales.

The MRR components

New is revenue from new customers, expansion is upgrades and add-ons from existing ones, contraction is downgrades, and churn is cancellations. Tracking each separately shows whether growth is coming from acquisition or from your existing base — and where the leaks are.

MRR to ARR

Annual Run Rate is simply ending MRR × 12 — a snapshot of annualized revenue at the current pace. It's useful for a headline number, but net new MRR trend is the real health signal. Watch it month over month alongside net revenue retention.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate MRR?
Add new and expansion MRR, subtract contraction and churned MRR to get net new MRR, then add it to your starting MRR for ending MRR.
What is net new MRR?
The net change in recurring revenue for the month: new plus expansion minus contraction minus churn.
How is ARR related to MRR?
Annual Run Rate equals ending MRR multiplied by 12 — the annualized value of your current recurring revenue.
