What is contribution margin?

Contribution margin is what's left from each sale after variable costs, available to cover fixed costs and then profit: CM = price − variable cost per unit. The CM ratio expresses it as a percentage of price. It tells you how much each additional unit sold actually contributes to the bottom line.

Contribution margin vs gross margin

Gross margin subtracts all cost of goods; contribution margin subtracts only variable costs, leaving fixed costs separate. That makes CM the right tool for pricing, break-even and "should we take this order" decisions, because it isolates the incremental economics of one more sale.

Finding break-even

Divide total fixed costs by contribution margin per unit to get break-even units — the volume where you stop losing money. Above it, every unit's full contribution margin drops to profit. Add your fixed costs above to see the break-even point in both units and revenue.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate contribution margin?
Subtract the variable cost per unit from the selling price. The result is what each unit contributes toward fixed costs and profit.
What's the difference between contribution margin and gross margin?
Gross margin subtracts all cost of goods sold; contribution margin subtracts only variable costs, keeping fixed costs separate.
How do I find break-even units?
Divide total fixed costs by the contribution margin per unit.
