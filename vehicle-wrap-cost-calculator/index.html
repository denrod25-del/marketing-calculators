How much does a vehicle wrap cost?

In 2026, a full wrap runs about $2,500–$4,000 for a car, $3,000–$5,500 for a pickup, $3,500–$7,000 for a van, and $5,000–$10,000+ for a box truck. Partial wraps start around $1,000–$2,000. With premium vinyl and pro installation, a wrap lasts 5–7 years.

Why wraps have the lowest CPM in advertising

A wrapped vehicle generates roughly 30,000–70,000 impressions a day and keeps working for years with no ongoing media spend. Spread a one-time cost over millions of lifetime impressions and the cost per 1,000 views (CPM) is often a few cents — far below any other channel. Even one extra job a month usually pays back the wrap many times over.

Frequently asked questions

How much does it cost to wrap a work van?
Typically $3,500–$7,000 for a full wrap depending on size (cargo vs Sprinter/transit). Partial wraps and lettering cost less.
How many people see a vehicle wrap?
About 30,000–70,000 impressions per day in a typical service area, which is why wraps have the cheapest cost per impression of any medium.
How long does a wrap last?
With premium vinyl and professional installation, 5–7 years or more — so the cost is spread across years of exposure.
