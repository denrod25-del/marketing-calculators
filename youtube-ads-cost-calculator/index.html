How much do YouTube ads cost?

YouTube ads average about $5–$10 CPM (cost per 1,000 impressions), or roughly $0.03–$0.12 per view for skippable in-stream. Shorts ads are cheaper at about a $4.85 CPM. You only pay for skippable ads when someone watches 30 seconds (or the whole ad) or clicks, so wasted spend is low.

Getting results on YouTube

Hook viewers in the first five seconds before the skip button appears, target by keyword, topic and audience, and always send clicks to a focused landing page. Retargeting site visitors on YouTube is cheap and effective. Measure by cost per lead and view-through conversions, not just views.

Frequently asked questions

How much do YouTube ads cost?
About $5–$10 CPM or $0.03–$0.12 per view for skippable in-stream. Shorts ads are cheaper. You pay only for watched or clicked skippable ads.
What is a good YouTube CPM?
$5–$10 is typical; non-skippable and premium placements run higher, Shorts run lower.
Are YouTube ads worth it for local business?
They can be — geo-target your area, keep videos short and hook fast, and retarget site visitors for the best cost per lead.
