How to calculate your break-even point

Break-even is where total revenue covers total cost — no profit, no loss. The formula is break-even units = fixed costs ÷ (price − variable cost per unit). The price minus variable cost is your contribution margin — the amount each sale contributes toward fixed costs. Once you pass break-even, that margin becomes profit.

Fixed vs variable costs

Fixed costs stay the same regardless of volume (rent, insurance, salaries, software). Variable costs rise with each unit or job (materials, subcontractor labor, fuel). Getting the split right is what makes the break-even accurate — and it shows whether to cut fixed overhead or improve per-job margin.

Using break-even to price and plan

Break-even tells you the minimum volume to survive and how a price change moves the goalpost. Add a target profit to see the volume needed to actually earn what you want. Raising price or trimming variable cost lifts your contribution margin, which lowers the units you need to break even.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate break-even point?
Divide fixed costs by the contribution margin per unit (price minus variable cost). The result is the units you must sell to cover costs.
What is contribution margin?
Price minus variable cost per unit — the amount each sale contributes toward fixed costs and, past break-even, profit.
How do I lower my break-even point?
Raise price, cut variable cost per unit, or reduce fixed overhead. Each increases margin or lowers the bar you must clear.
