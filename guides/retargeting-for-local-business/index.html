Retargeting for Local Business

Most visitors leave without calling. Retargeting gives you a second chance at them.

What retargeting does

Retargeting shows ads to people who already visited your site but didn't call or book. Because they've heard of you, these ads convert far better and cheaper than cold advertising — you're nudging warm prospects to finish what they started, not introducing yourself from scratch.

How it works

A small tracking pixel on your site (from Meta, Google or others) builds an audience of visitors. You then run ads to just that audience across social feeds, websites and YouTube. Anyone who bounced from your booking page can be met with a reminder ad hours later.

What to show

Keep it simple and reassuring: a reminder of who you are, a strong trust cue (reviews, guarantee, license), and a clear offer or call to action. Segment if you can — someone who viewed "water heater repair" should see a water-heater ad, not a generic one.

Cap the frequency

Retargeting done heavy-handed feels like being followed. Set frequency caps so people see your ad a few times, not dozens, and exclude anyone who already converted. A light, well-timed presence builds trust; an overwhelming one annoys.

Measure incremental value

Judge retargeting on incremental conversions — jobs it produced that wouldn't have happened otherwise — not just last-click credit, since some visitors would have returned anyway. Track cost per booked job and keep budgets sized to your actual return-visitor volume.

Frequently asked questions

What is retargeting?
Showing ads to people who already visited your website but didn't convert. Because they know you, these ads convert better and cheaper than cold ads.
How do I set up retargeting?
Add a tracking pixel from Meta, Google or another network to your site to build an audience of visitors, then run ads to just that audience.
How often should retargeting ads show?
Use frequency caps so people see them a few times, not constantly, and exclude anyone who already booked. Light and well-timed beats overwhelming.
