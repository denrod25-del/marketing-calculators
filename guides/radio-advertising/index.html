Radio Advertising for Local Business

Radio still reaches your whole town on the commute. Here's how to make it pay.

Why radio still works

Radio reaches a huge local audience during commutes and workdays, and it's a trusted, familiar medium. For a local service business, a well-run radio campaign builds broad awareness across your whole market — the kind of top-of-mind familiarity that makes people call you first when something breaks.

AM/FM vs streaming audio

Broadcast AM/FM still has the widest local reach, especially in cars. Streaming audio and digital radio add precise targeting and measurement. Many businesses combine both: broadcast for reach, streaming for targeting. Match the mix to where your customers actually listen.

Pick stations and dayparts

Choose stations whose audience matches your customers, and buy the dayparts (time slots) when they're listening — morning and evening drive time reach the most commuters. Ask stations for audience data; don't pay drive-time rates for a slot your customers never hear.

Write a spot that sticks

You have 30 or 60 seconds and no visuals. Lead with the problem you solve, repeat your name and phone number (people can't scroll back), keep it simple, and end with one clear call to action. A memorable, repeated phone number or tagline does the heavy lifting.

Frequency is everything

One ad is forgotten instantly. Radio works through repetition — the same listeners hearing your spot many times. It's better to run enough frequency on one well-chosen station than to spread a thin buy across several. Commit to a schedule with real repetition or don't bother.

Cost and measurement

Radio is priced per spot by station reach and daypart. Track it with a dedicated phone number, a promo code, or a "heard us on the radio" question, and watch for lifts in calls and branded searches. Judge it as an awareness driver over weeks, not days.

Frequently asked questions

Does radio advertising still work for local businesses?
Yes — it reaches a broad local audience during commutes and builds top-of-mind awareness. It works best with enough frequency on well-chosen stations.
How much does radio advertising cost?
It's priced per spot based on the station's reach and the daypart. Drive-time slots cost more; ask stations for audience data to judge value.
How do I measure radio results?
Use a dedicated phone number or promo code, ask new customers how they heard of you, and watch for lifts in calls and branded searches.
