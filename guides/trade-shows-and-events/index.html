Trade Shows & Local Events

Home shows and community events put you face-to-face with ready buyers. Here's how to profit.

Why events still work

In a digital world, face-to-face still converts. Home shows, fairs and community events put you in front of people who are actively planning projects, and a handshake builds trust no ad can. For local service businesses, the right event can seed months of work.

Pick the right events

Not every event is worth it. Choose ones your actual customers attend — home and garden shows, neighborhood festivals, real-estate or new-homeowner events. Ask organizers for attendance numbers and demographics, and weigh the booth cost against realistic booked jobs before committing.

A booth that draws people

Give people a reason to stop: a clear banner stating what you do, something visual or interactive, and a simple giveaway or offer. Friendly staff who ask questions beat a table of brochures. You have seconds to signal "this is relevant to me."

Capture leads, don't just chat

Conversations you don't record are lost. Use a signup sheet, a tablet form, or a QR code to a booking page, and offer an incentive to share contact details (a discount, a free estimate, a prize draw). Every visitor should leave a way to follow up.

Follow up fast

Most event ROI is made in the days after, not at the booth. Contact every lead within 48 hours while you're fresh in their mind, reference the event, and make it easy to book. Speed here is the difference between a full pipeline and a stack of forgotten cards.

Measure the return

Track booth and staffing costs against leads captured and jobs booked so you know which events to repeat. Tag event leads separately and follow them through to revenue — an event that looks expensive can be your best channel once you count the jobs it produces.

Frequently asked questions

Are trade shows and home shows worth it for local businesses?
They can be, when you choose events your customers attend, capture leads systematically, and follow up fast. Measure cost against booked jobs to know.
How do I capture leads at an event?
Use a signup sheet, tablet form or QR code to a booking page, and offer an incentive like a free estimate or prize draw to collect contact details.
When should I follow up with event leads?
Within 48 hours, while you're fresh in their mind. Most event ROI comes from prompt follow-up, not the conversation at the booth.
