Local SEO Fundamentals

The map pack is one piece. Here's the full local SEO foundation that gets you found.

What local SEO covers

Local SEO is everything that helps you show up when nearby people search for your service — the map pack, yes, but also the organic results below it. It rests on three pillars: an optimized Google Business Profile, a solid website, and consistent, trusted signals across the web.

On-page basics

Each important page needs a clear title and heading with your service and city, helpful content that answers the searcher, and a strong call to action. Don't stuff keywords — write for people, include your location naturally, and make sure every page has a clear purpose and next step.

Build location and service pages

Create a dedicated page for each core service and each area you serve ("[service] in [city]"). One generic page can't rank for everything. Distinct, genuinely useful pages let you target many local searches — just make each one unique, not a copy with the town name swapped.

Citations and NAP consistency

Your Name, Address and Phone must match exactly everywhere — your site, Google, Bing, Apple, Yelp and directories. Inconsistent listings confuse search engines and drag rankings. Get listed in a handful of quality local and industry directories, and clean up duplicates.

Earn local links

Links from local and relevant sites signal trust. Sponsor a local team or event, join the chamber of commerce, partner with complementary businesses, or get featured in local press. A few genuine local links often outweigh dozens of low-quality ones.

Technical and mobile foundations

Make sure your site loads fast, works flawlessly on phones, uses HTTPS, and has clean, crawlable pages with LocalBusiness schema. These basics don't win on their own, but getting them wrong quietly caps everything else you do.

Frequently asked questions

What is local SEO?
Everything that helps your business show up when nearby people search for your service — your Google Business Profile, your website's on-page content, and consistent trusted signals across the web.
Do I need separate pages for each service and city?
Yes — distinct, genuinely useful pages for each core service and area rank far better than one generic page trying to cover everything.
Why does NAP consistency matter?
Inconsistent Name, Address and Phone data across the web confuses search engines and drags your local rankings down.
