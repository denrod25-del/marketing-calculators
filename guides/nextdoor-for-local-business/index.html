Nextdoor for Local Service Businesses

Verified neighbors asking "who's a good plumber?" — Nextdoor is built for local word of mouth.

Why Nextdoor works for local services

Nextdoor users are verified neighbors, and a huge share of activity is people asking for local recommendations. That makes it hyper-local and trust-driven — a recommendation there converts a lot like a referral. For home and service businesses, it's one of the highest-intent local channels.

Start with the free Business Page

Claim a free Business Page so you show up when neighbors search or get recommended. Keep it complete and current — hours, services, photos — because recommendations point back to it.

Win the recommendations

When a neighbor asks "who does anyone recommend for [service]?" those threads drive real jobs. Encourage happy customers to recommend you by name, and respond helpfully (not spammy) when you're mentioned. Organic recommendations are the heart of the platform.

Local Deals and ads

Local Deals let you post an offer to nearby neighbors cheaply, and the self-serve Ads Manager runs a CPM/CPC auction to reach a wider local audience. Typical CPMs run about $10–$20 and CPCs around $2.50–$3.50 — affordable for a local business.

Etiquette that keeps you welcome

  • Be genuinely helpful, not salesy, in neighborhood threads.
  • Don't spam multiple neighborhoods with the same post.
  • Respond promptly and politely to questions.
  • Let real recommendations do the heavy lifting; use ads to amplify.

Frequently asked questions

Is Nextdoor good for local businesses?
Yes, especially home and service businesses — the audience is verified neighbors with high local intent, so recommendations convert like referrals.
How much do Nextdoor ads cost?
Roughly $10–$20 CPM or $2.50–$3.50 per click via Ads Manager; Local Deals can start at just a few dollars.
Do I need to pay to use Nextdoor?
No — a free Business Page and neighbor recommendations drive organic leads; ads simply amplify reach.
