Podcast Advertising for Local Business

Podcast listeners trust their hosts. A host-read plug can carry that trust to you.

Why podcast ads convert

Podcast listeners are loyal and engaged, and they trust the hosts they listen to weekly. When a host personally recommends a business, that trust transfers. That's why podcast ads — especially host-read ones — often convert better than their raw audience size suggests.

Host-read vs produced

Host-read ads, where the host talks about you in their own voice, feel like a personal recommendation and perform best. Produced spots (pre-recorded) are cheaper and more consistent but less personal. For local businesses, a genuine host-read on the right show is usually worth the premium.

Find the right shows

Look for podcasts your customers listen to — local news and community shows, or niche shows tied to home, DIY or your region. Audience fit matters far more than size; a small, tightly relevant local podcast can outperform a huge general one.

Local and niche podcasts

Many cities have local podcasts, and niche shows reach specific interests cheaply. These smaller shows often have devoted audiences and lower rates, and hosts who'll genuinely champion a local sponsor. That combination is where local advertisers get the best value.

Cost and CPMs

Podcast ads are typically priced on CPM (cost per thousand listens), often in the $18–$50 range depending on show size and ad type, with host-reads at the higher end. Small local shows may sell flat sponsorships. Weigh cost against how well the audience matches your customers.

Measure with codes

Give each show a unique promo code or vanity URL so you can attribute leads. Ask new customers where they heard you, and track the shows that produce booked jobs. Attribution is podcasting's weak spot — codes and dedicated links are how you fix it.

Frequently asked questions

Do podcast ads work for local businesses?
They can — listeners trust their hosts, so host-read ads on well-matched local or niche shows often convert above what the audience size suggests.
How much do podcast ads cost?
Typically $18–$50 CPM depending on show size and ad type, with host-read spots at the higher end; small local shows may sell flat sponsorships.
How do I track podcast ad results?
Use a unique promo code or vanity URL per show and ask new customers where they heard you, so you can attribute leads to specific podcasts.
