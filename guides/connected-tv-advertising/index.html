Connected TV & Streaming Ads

TV advertising used to be for big brands. Streaming put it within reach of local businesses.

What connected TV is

Connected TV (CTV) is advertising on streaming services watched on smart TVs and devices — the ad breaks in ad-supported streaming apps. Unlike old broadcast TV, it's bought digitally, targeted precisely, and measurable, which is what finally makes TV viable for a local budget.

Why it beats traditional TV for locals

Broadcast TV blankets a whole region and charges accordingly. CTV lets you target just your service area, specific demographics, and even household types, so you pay to reach the people who can actually hire you — not an entire market that mostly can't.

Targeting your area

Set a geographic radius or ZIP list around your service area and layer on relevant demographics like homeowners. This precision is the whole point: a local plumber can put a TV-quality ad in front of nearby homeowners without paying big-market broadcast rates.

Creative that works on the big screen

CTV ads are usually 15 or 30 seconds and can't be skipped, so make them count. Lead with the problem you solve, show real work or team, keep branding and your call to action clear, and make sure it looks sharp on a large screen. You don't need a Hollywood budget — clarity beats polish.

What it costs

CTV is priced on CPM (cost per thousand views), commonly in the $25–$65 range depending on targeting and inventory. Because the ad is unskippable and fully viewed, that CPM buys real attention. Set a monthly budget and treat it as an awareness investment measured over time.

Measuring CTV

CTV is more measurable than broadcast but less direct than search. Track lifts in branded searches, direct traffic and calls after campaigns launch, use a vanity URL or offer code, and watch completion rates. Judge it as an awareness driver that supports your direct-response channels.
