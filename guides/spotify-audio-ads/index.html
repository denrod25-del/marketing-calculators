Audio Ads Guide

Spotify & Streaming Audio Ads

Streaming audio puts a targeted ad in listeners' ears across your whole service area.

What streaming audio ads are

Platforms like Spotify serve audio ads between songs to listeners on the free tier. Unlike broadcast radio, they're bought digitally with precise targeting and full measurement — you reach specific people in your area, not a whole region, and you can see exactly what you got.

Target your area

Set a location around your service area and layer on age, interests or listening context (workout, commute, focus playlists). This precision means a local business can run audio ads without wasting budget on listeners far outside where you work.

Audio vs video ads

Audio ads play between tracks and often pair with a clickable companion banner. Spotify also offers video ad formats. Audio is the workhorse — cheap, targeted and reaching people while their hands are busy — with the banner giving a tap-to-act option.

Creative that works in audio

You have around 15–30 seconds and no guaranteed visual. Lead with the problem, say your name clearly and more than once, keep it conversational, and end with a simple call to action or memorable URL. The companion banner should reinforce the same message and offer a click.

Cost and CPMs

Streaming audio is priced on CPM, commonly around $15–$25, with low minimum budgets that suit small businesses. Because targeting is tight, that CPM reaches a genuinely relevant local audience. Set a monthly budget and treat it as targeted awareness.

Measurement

Streaming audio is far more measurable than broadcast: track impressions, companion-banner clicks, and reach. Add a promo code or vanity URL to tie it to leads, and watch for lifts in branded search and direct calls. Judge it over a few weeks alongside your direct-response channels.
