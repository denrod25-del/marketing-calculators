Google Ads for Contractors

Someone typing "emergency plumber near me" is ready to hire. Here's how to capture that call profitably.

What Google Search Ads do for local trades

Search Ads put you at the top of results the moment someone searches for your service. Unlike social, you're reaching people with active intent — they need the job done now. For the trades, that intent is why paid search, done right, is one of the most reliable lead sources you can buy.

Start with high-intent keywords

Bid on terms that signal a ready buyer: "[service] near me," "emergency [service]," "[service] in [city]," and specific problems ("water heater replacement"). Skip broad, research-style terms that burn budget. A tight list of commercial keywords beats a huge list of vague ones.

Negative keywords save your budget

Negative keywords stop you paying for irrelevant clicks. Add terms like "jobs," "salary," "DIY," "free," "how to," and parts you don't service. Review the search-terms report weekly and keep adding negatives — it's the single biggest lever on wasted spend.

Location and schedule targeting

Limit ads to your true service area by radius or ZIP, and bid up where your best jobs come from. If you take after-hours emergency calls, run those hours; if you don't, pause ads when you can't answer. A missed call from a paid click is money thrown away.

The landing page decides your ROI

Send clicks to a focused page that matches the search — not your homepage. It needs a clear headline, a big tap-to-call phone number, trust signals (license, reviews), and a short form. A great landing page can double your conversion rate on the exact same ad spend.

Track cost per booked job, not per click

Clicks and even leads don't pay the bills — booked jobs do. Use call tracking and conversion tracking to tie spend to real jobs, then judge campaigns on cost per booked job against your average ticket. That's the number that tells you whether to scale or cut.

Frequently asked questions

Are Google Ads worth it for contractors?
Yes, when you target high-intent local keywords, use negative keywords, send clicks to a focused landing page, and track cost per booked job against your average ticket.
How much should a contractor spend on Google Ads?
Start with a budget you can sustain for a few months, enough to gather data — often a few thousand a month in competitive trades — then scale what produces profitable booked jobs.
What's the difference between Google Ads and Local Services Ads?
Local Services Ads charge per lead and show the Google Guaranteed badge; standard Search Ads charge per click and give you more control over keywords and landing pages. Many trades run both.
