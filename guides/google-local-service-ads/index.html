Google Local Services Ads: Complete Guide

The best pay-per-lead channel for the trades — how LSAs work and how to win with them.

What Local Services Ads are

LSAs are Google's pay-per-lead ads that appear at the very top of local searches, above regular Search ads. Each listing shows your rating, reviews and the green Google Guaranteed badge. You're charged per lead — a call or message — not per click.

The Google Guaranteed badge

To run LSAs you pass a license, insurance and background check, which earns the Google Guaranteed badge. That badge is a trust signal that lifts response, and Google backs qualifying jobs up to a coverage limit, giving homeowners extra confidence to choose you.

How pricing works

You pay per lead. Home-services leads average around $53 in 2026, roughly $40–$90 for plumbing, $60–$120 for HVAC and $30–$75 for electrical, with competitive metros higher. Set a weekly budget and Google fills it with leads. Because you pay per lead (not click), cost per booked job is often lower than traditional PPC.

Ranking in LSAs

Rank is driven by review score and volume, responsiveness (answer fast — missed calls hurt), proximity to the searcher, and your Google Guaranteed status and hours. Fast response and a steady stream of reviews are the two biggest levers.

Disputing bad leads

You can dispute leads that are spam, wrong service, or out of your area, and Google credits valid disputes so you aren't charged. Review your leads weekly and dispute promptly to keep your effective cost per lead down.

Tips to get the most from LSAs

  • Answer every call — speed and pickup rate drive rank and conversion.
  • Keep collecting reviews; they compound your ranking.
  • Set accurate service areas and hours.
  • Dispute invalid leads every week.
  • Track cost per booked job, not just cost per lead.
