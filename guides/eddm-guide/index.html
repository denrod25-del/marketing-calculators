EDDM: Targeting the Right ZIP Routes

Blanket a neighborhood with mail — no list required. Here's how to do it well.

What EDDM is

Every Door Direct Mail is a USPS program that lets you mail to every address on chosen carrier routes without buying a mailing list or knowing names. You pick postal routes on a map, print postcards, and drop them at the post office. It's the cheapest way to saturate a specific area.

How it works

Choose carrier routes by ZIP, filter by basic demographics (age, income, household size), print EDDM-compliant postcards, bundle them per USPS rules, and drop them at the local post office. No addresses or list rental needed — you're mailing the whole route.

What EDDM costs

All-in cost is typically $0.30–$0.55 per piece for saturation postcards — cheaper than targeted mail because it skips list rental and qualifies for the lowest postage tier. A 5,000-piece EDDM drop runs roughly $1,500–$2,750 depending on size and stock.

Choose the right routes

The targeting lives in route selection. Pick neighborhoods that match your best customers — for home services, older housing stock, higher home values, or areas with a known issue (hard water, aging pipes). Concentrate on routes near recent jobs so your trucks and reputation reinforce the mail.

Design that gets a response

  • Lead with a clear, specific offer (a discount or free assessment).
  • Big headline, big phone number, and a deadline to prompt action.
  • Use both sides; make the offer obvious at a glance.
  • Include a trust cue — license, years in business, or a rating.
  • Add a QR code to book or claim the offer instantly.

EDDM vs targeted mail

EDDM is best for saturation and awareness in defined neighborhoods at the lowest cost. If you need to reach a specific list — past customers, a demographic slice, or a purchased list — targeted mail costs more per piece but hits exactly who you choose. Many businesses use EDDM for prospecting and targeted mail for reactivation.

Frequently asked questions

How much does EDDM cost?
Typically $0.30–$0.55 per piece all-in for saturation postcards, cheaper than targeted mail because it skips list rental and uses the lowest postage tier.
Do I need a mailing list for EDDM?
No — you select USPS carrier routes on a map and mail every address on them, so no names or list rental are required.
When should I use EDDM vs targeted mail?
Use EDDM to saturate a neighborhood cheaply; use targeted mail when you need to reach a specific list like past customers.
