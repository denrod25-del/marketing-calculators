Facebook & Instagram Ads for Local Business

Meta's local targeting puts your offer in front of nearby homeowners for pennies per view.

Why Meta ads work for local business

Facebook and Instagram let you reach specific neighborhoods cheaply and show up where people already spend time. The intent is lower than search — they're not actively looking — so the job is to create demand and build familiarity: seasonal offers, before-and-after work, and reminders that you exist before they need you.

Pick the right objective

Match the campaign objective to your goal. For direct response, use Leads (instant forms) or Sales/Conversions pointed at your site. Use Traffic or Engagement only for awareness. The wrong objective tells Meta's algorithm to optimize for the wrong action and wastes budget.

Local audience targeting

Start simple: a radius around your service area plus basic filters like homeowners or age. Meta's own delivery finds the right people within that area better than over-complex targeting. Layer interests lightly, and build a lookalike from your customer list once you have enough data.

Creative that stops the scroll

On social, the creative is the campaign. Use real photos and short vertical video of your actual work — before-and-afters, the team, a quick tip. Authentic beats polished. Lead with a clear offer or hook in the first second, and keep captions short with an obvious call to action.

Lead forms vs landing pages

Instant lead forms convert well because they never leave the app, but leads can be lower-quality and need fast follow-up. Landing pages ask more of the user but attract more serious prospects. Test both, and whichever you use, call new leads within minutes — speed-to-lead makes or breaks social leads.

Budget and measurement

Start small, give each campaign enough budget to exit the learning phase, and judge results on cost per lead and cost per booked job — not likes. Let winners run, cut losers, and refresh creative every few weeks as performance fatigues.

Frequently asked questions

Do Facebook ads work for local service businesses?
Yes, especially for demand generation and seasonal offers. Intent is lower than search, so lead with strong creative and a clear local offer, and follow up on leads fast.
How much do Facebook ads cost for a local business?
Many local businesses run effective campaigns on modest daily budgets. Costs vary by area and audience; judge results on cost per booked job rather than impressions.
Should I use lead forms or send people to my website?
Instant forms convert more but can be lower quality; landing pages attract more serious prospects. Test both and respond to every lead within minutes.
