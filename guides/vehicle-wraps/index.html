Vehicle Wraps That Actually Get Calls

A wrapped truck is the cheapest advertising per impression — if people can actually read it.

Why wraps work

A wrapped vehicle produces roughly 30,000–70,000 impressions a day and keeps working for years with no ongoing media spend. Spread a one-time cost over millions of lifetime views and the cost per 1,000 impressions is often just a few cents — the lowest CPM in advertising.

The design rules that get calls

Most wraps fail because they're cluttered. People see your vehicle for about three seconds. Design for that:

  • One clear headline of what you do ("Plumbing & Water Heaters").
  • A big, legible phone number — the single most important element.
  • High contrast and large fonts readable from 50+ feet.
  • Your logo, and your license number for trust.
  • Leave whitespace — don't fill every inch.

What to include (and skip)

Include: what you do, phone number, website, and a trust cue (license #, "licensed & insured," or a rating). Skip: long paragraphs, tiny social icons, and a list of every service. A passing driver needs the offer and the number, nothing more.

Full vs partial wrap

A full wrap maximizes impact and covers the whole vehicle; a partial wrap or professional lettering costs far less and still delivers the essentials. For a work truck that parks at job sites all day, even lettering plus a bold phone number performs well.

Cost and lifespan

In 2026, full wraps run about $2,500–$4,000 for a car, $3,000–$5,500 for a pickup, $3,500–$7,000 for a van, and $5,000–$10,000+ for a box truck. With premium vinyl and pro installation, a wrap lasts 5–7 years — so amortized cost per month is small.

Measure the results

Use a dedicated phone number or a simple "where did you hear about us?" question so wrap-driven calls get counted. Park where your customers are — job sites, busy lots, and your service-area neighborhoods.

Frequently asked questions

How much does a vehicle wrap cost?
About $2,500–$4,000 for a car, $3,000–$5,500 for a pickup, $3,500–$7,000 for a van, and $5,000–$10,000+ for a box truck.
What should a vehicle wrap include?
What you do, a big legible phone number, your website, a trust cue like a license number, and plenty of whitespace.
How long does a wrap last?
5–7 years or more with premium vinyl and professional installation, so the cost spreads across years of exposure.
