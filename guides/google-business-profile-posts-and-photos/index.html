Google Business Profile: Posts, Photos & Q&A

Ranking gets you seen; your profile content gets you chosen. Here's how to work every feature.

Your profile is a conversion page

Ranking in the map pack gets you the view — but the profile itself decides whether they call. Photos, posts, Q&A and reviews all shape that split-second choice. Treating your Google Business Profile as a living conversion page, not a static listing, is what turns visibility into jobs.

Post regularly

Google Posts let you share offers, updates and tips that show on your profile. Fresh posts signal an active business and give prospects a reason to choose you now. A simple weekly or biweekly post — a seasonal tip, a current offer — keeps the profile alive.

Photos do heavy lifting

Profiles with strong, current photos get far more clicks and calls. Add real images of your team, trucks, and completed work, and refresh them monthly. Authentic photos build trust and help you stand out from competitors using stock images or none at all.

Own your Q&A

Anyone can ask — or answer — questions on your profile, so seed it yourself with the questions customers actually ask ("Do you offer emergency service?") and answer them clearly. Monitor for new questions and respond fast, before someone else answers wrong.

Use products, services and messaging

Fill the services and products sections with keyword-aligned descriptions, and turn on messaging if you can answer quickly. Every populated section gives Google more to match on and gives prospects more reasons to act without leaving the profile.

Track profile insights

Use the profile's performance data — calls, direction requests, website clicks, and search terms — to see what's working. Double down on the photos, posts and services that drive the most actions, and keep refining.

Frequently asked questions

Do Google Business Profile posts help?
Yes — regular posts signal an active business, surface offers and tips on your profile, and give prospects a reason to choose you now.
How many photos should I add to my profile?
As many quality, current ones as you can — team, trucks and completed work — and refresh them monthly. Photo-rich profiles get more clicks and calls.
Can I answer questions on my own profile?
Yes, and you should — seed the common questions customers ask and answer them clearly before anyone else answers incorrectly.
