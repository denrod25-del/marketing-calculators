Reputation Guide

A Review-Generation System That Works

Reviews rank you in the map pack and win the click. Here's a system to earn them consistently.

Why reviews matter so much

Reviews feed the "prominence" factor that ranks you in the map pack, and they're the deciding trust signal for a homeowner choosing who to call. Most people now only consider businesses with 4.5 stars or more, and recency counts — a wall of old reviews is weaker than a steady, fresh stream.

Ask at the moment of delight

The best time to ask is right after a great job, while the customer is happy. Train your team to ask in person ("Would you mind leaving us a quick review?") and follow up with a text or email that contains your direct review link.

Make it one tap

Every extra step loses reviews. Send your Google review link directly so the customer lands on the review box in one tap. A short SMS usually outperforms email for review requests because it's read within minutes.

A simple request template

"Hi [name] — thanks for trusting us with your [service]! A quick Google review helps other neighbors find us. It takes 30 seconds: [your review link]. Thank you!" Personalize it and send within a day of the job.

Respond to every review

Reply to all reviews, positive and negative, within a day or two. Thank happy customers by name; for a negative review, respond calmly, take it offline, and fix the issue. Public, professional responses show prospects how you handle problems.

What Google prohibits

  • Don't buy or fake reviews.
  • Don't "gate" reviews (only asking happy customers via a filter) — Google prohibits it.
  • Don't offer payment for reviews.
  • Do ask everyone, make it easy, and let honest feedback flow.

Frequently asked questions

How do I get more Google reviews?
Ask every customer at the moment of delight, send a one-tap review link (SMS works best), and follow up. Consistency beats intensity.
Can I offer a discount for a review?
No — paying or incentivizing reviews violates Google's policies and can get reviews removed or your profile penalized.
Should I respond to negative reviews?
Yes. Respond calmly and professionally, take it offline, and fix the issue. It shows prospects how you handle problems.
