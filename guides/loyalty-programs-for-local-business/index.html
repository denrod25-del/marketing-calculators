Customer Loyalty Programs

Keeping a customer costs far less than finding a new one. A loyalty plan makes them stay.

Why loyalty pays

Repeat customers cost far less to sell to, spend more over time, and refer others. A loyalty program formalizes the relationship so customers keep coming back to you instead of shopping around. For service businesses, that recurring, predictable work is some of the most profitable revenue you can build.

Program types that fit local

Keep it appropriate to your business: a points or visit-based program for frequent services, a simple VIP tier with priority scheduling and perks, or a membership with a recurring fee. The best structure is the one your customers understand instantly and actually use.

Maintenance plans for the trades

For home services, a maintenance plan is loyalty gold: an annual fee or subscription covering regular tune-ups, priority service and member discounts. It locks in recurring revenue, keeps you top of mind, creates natural touchpoints, and turns one-off customers into long-term relationships.

What to reward

Reward the behaviors you want more of: repeat bookings, referrals, reviews (where allowed) and prepaid plans. Make the reward feel worthwhile — a meaningful discount, priority service, or a free seasonal check — not a token that no one values.

Keep it simple

Complexity kills loyalty programs. If customers can't explain how it works in a sentence, it's too complicated. Make earning and redeeming effortless, communicate the value clearly, and don't bury the benefit behind fine print.

Measure retention and value

Track repeat purchase rate, customer lifespan and the lifetime value of members versus non-members. A good program should visibly lift how often customers return and how long they stay — those numbers tell you it's working and worth the cost.

Frequently asked questions

Do loyalty programs work for local service businesses?
Yes — they drive repeat work, which is cheaper and more profitable than new acquisition. Maintenance plans are especially effective for the trades.
What is a maintenance plan?
A recurring membership covering regular tune-ups, priority service and member discounts — locking in recurring revenue and keeping you top of mind.
How do I keep a loyalty program simple?
If customers can't explain it in one sentence, it's too complex. Make earning and redeeming effortless and communicate the value clearly.
