SMS Text Marketing for Local Business

Texts get read in minutes. For appointments and reminders, nothing beats SMS.

Why SMS works for service businesses

Text messages are opened within minutes and read at rates email can't touch. For time-sensitive things — appointment confirmations, "tech is on the way," reminders and quick offers — SMS is the most reliable channel to actually reach a customer.

Get consent first

SMS marketing requires clear opt-in consent under rules like the TCPA. Collect permission at booking or via a keyword sign-up, keep records, and include opt-out instructions ("reply STOP") in messages. Skipping consent risks real penalties — treat it as non-negotiable.

What to send

Lead with useful, transactional texts: booking confirmations, reminders, on-the-way alerts and follow-ups. Sprinkle in occasional value — a seasonal reminder or a limited offer. Keep it short, identify your business, and always give a clear next step.

Timing and frequency

Send during reasonable daytime hours and only when there's a reason. SMS is intimate — over-texting gets you muted or reported fast. A handful of well-timed, relevant messages beats a steady drip of promotions.

Combine with your other channels

Use SMS for urgency and email for depth. A reminder text can nudge someone to act on an email offer; a confirmation text can carry a link to reschedule or review. Together they cover both immediacy and detail.

Measure it

Track opt-in growth, replies, click-throughs on any links, and — most importantly — jobs booked or rebooked from texts. Because SMS costs a few cents per message, even modest response rates deliver strong ROI when the messages are relevant.

Frequently asked questions

Is SMS marketing effective for local businesses?
Very — texts are read within minutes, making SMS ideal for confirmations, reminders and time-sensitive offers, with strong ROI at a few cents per message.
Do I need consent to text customers?
Yes. SMS marketing requires clear opt-in consent under rules like the TCPA, with records kept and easy opt-out in every message.
How often should I text customers?
Only when there's a real reason, during daytime hours. Over-texting gets you muted or reported, so keep it relevant and infrequent.
