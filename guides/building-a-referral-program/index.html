Building a Referral Program That Works

Referred customers close easier and stay longer. Here's how to make referrals systematic.

Why referrals are your best leads

Referred customers arrive pre-trusted, close at higher rates, haggle less and refer others. Referral leads are usually the cheapest and highest-converting you can get. The problem is most businesses leave them to chance — a real program makes them systematic.

Decide what to offer

Pick an incentive that motivates without feeling cheap: a gift card, account credit, a discount on the next service, or a donation. Consider a double-sided reward — something for the referrer and the new customer — which lifts participation on both ends.

Ask at the right moment

The best time to ask is right after a great result, while goodwill is high. Train your team to mention it in person, then follow up with a text or email that includes an easy way to share. A referral ask attached to a review request works well.

Make it effortless

Every extra step costs referrals. Give customers a simple link, code or card to hand off, and make redemption obvious for the new customer. The easier it is to share and claim, the more it happens.

Track and thank

Use a code or a simple "who referred you?" field so you can attribute and reward referrals reliably. Always thank the referrer promptly — recognition keeps them referring. Watch your referral rate and the revenue it drives to know it's working.

Keep it honest

Be transparent about rewards, honor them quickly, and avoid incentives tied to online reviews (which platforms prohibit). A referral program rewards sending customers, not buying five-star ratings — keep those separate to stay compliant.

Frequently asked questions

How do I start a referral program?
Choose a motivating incentive, ask happy customers right after a great result, make sharing effortless with a link or code, and track referrals so you can reward them.
Should I reward both the referrer and the new customer?
Double-sided rewards often lift participation, since both parties have a reason to take part. Test what works for your margins.
Can I reward customers for online reviews?
No — incentivizing reviews violates platform rules. Keep referral rewards (for sending customers) separate from reviews.
