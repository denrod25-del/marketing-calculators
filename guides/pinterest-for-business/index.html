Pinterest for Home & Service Businesses

Pinterest is where homeowners plan projects. Get found before they even call around.

Why Pinterest is a search engine

Pinterest behaves less like social media and more like a visual search engine. People go there to plan — remodels, projects, home improvements — and search for ideas. That intent, plus content that lives and keeps getting found for months, makes it a quiet lead source for home and service businesses.

Reach planners early

Pinterest users are often at the planning stage, before they've chosen who to hire. Getting your work in front of them then plants your name early in the decision. For project-based trades — remodels, landscaping, renovations — that early influence is valuable.

Pin your projects

Create pins from your best finished work: high-quality vertical images, before-and-afters, and project galleries. Pinterest is visual first, so image quality matters. Organize pins into themed boards (Kitchens, Bathrooms, Outdoor) that match how people search.

Keywords and rich pins

Because it's search-driven, keywords matter: write descriptive, keyword-rich pin titles and descriptions, and name boards clearly. Enable rich pins so your pins pull live info from your site. This is how the right searches find your work.

Drive traffic to booking

Every pin should link back to a relevant page on your site — a project, a service, a booking form. Pinterest is one of the better social platforms for referral traffic precisely because pins carry links and keep sending visitors long after you post them.

Pinterest ads

Promoted pins can extend reach to people searching your categories, often at low cost. Because the audience is already in planning mode, promoted content can catch high-intent prospects. Start small, target locally where possible, and track clicks through to leads.
