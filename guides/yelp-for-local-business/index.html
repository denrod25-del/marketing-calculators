Yelp for Local Service Businesses

Love it or not, Yelp ranks high and shapes first impressions. Here's how to work it.

Why Yelp still matters

Yelp pages rank near the top of many local searches and feed into Apple Maps and Siri results. Even if you never spend a dollar there, prospects will find and read your Yelp page — so a claimed, complete, well-reviewed profile is worth having regardless of how you feel about the platform.

Claim and complete your page

Claim your free business page and fill everything: services, hours, service area, photos, license and a clear description. A complete page with strong photos converts far better than a bare listing, and Yelp favors active, complete profiles.

Understand the review filter

Yelp's recommendation software hides reviews it judges less established — often from new or inactive accounts. That's why genuine reviews sometimes disappear. You can't game it: the fix is a steady flow of reviews from real, active Yelp users over time, not a burst of one-off accounts.

Never ask for reviews the wrong way

Yelp explicitly discourages directly soliciting reviews and forbids incentivizing them. Instead, add a "Find us on Yelp" cue in your shop, on invoices and your site, and let active Yelpers review naturally. Focus your active review requests on Google, where asking is allowed.

Respond to every review

Reply to reviews publicly and professionally — thank positive ones and address negative ones calmly, then take the detail offline. Prospects read your responses as much as the reviews; a measured reply to a bad review can win more trust than the complaint costs you.

Are Yelp ads worth it?

Yelp ads can work in categories where buyers actively browse Yelp, but costs and lead quality vary and contracts can be rigid. Start small, track cost per booked job, and only scale if the numbers hold. For many trades, a strong free profile plus Google is the better core.
