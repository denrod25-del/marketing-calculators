Geofencing Marketing

Draw a virtual fence around a neighborhood or competitor and advertise to phones inside it.

What geofencing is

Geofencing draws a virtual boundary around a physical area and serves mobile ads to phones inside it. It turns location into targeting — you reach people because of where they are, which is uniquely powerful for local businesses tied to a service area.

How it works

You define areas on a map — neighborhoods, event venues, even competitors' locations — and people who enter see your ads in apps and on websites, sometimes for a window afterward. It runs on the same mobile ad networks as other display, just triggered by location.

Smart targeting ideas

Fence the neighborhoods you want more work in, areas with a known issue you fix, new-housing developments, or venues where your customers gather. Some businesses even fence competitors to reach people already shopping the category — check platform rules, but location targeting opens creative options.

Creative and offers

Mobile ads are small, so keep them simple: a clear offer, your name, and a tap-to-call or booking action. A location-relevant hook ("Serving [neighborhood] today") plus a reason to act now works best on a phone screen.

What it costs

Geofencing is typically bought on CPM, often in the $6–$15 range depending on how tightly you target. Precise fences cost more per thousand but waste less, since nearly everyone reached is genuinely local. Set a budget matched to how many people actually pass through your zones.

Measuring results

Track clicks and calls from the ads, use a dedicated number or landing page, and where available, foot-traffic or conversion-zone reporting that shows whether people who saw the ad later visited. As with most awareness tactics, judge it on calls and jobs over a few weeks, not a single day.
