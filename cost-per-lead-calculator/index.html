What is cost per lead?

Cost per lead (CPL) is your marketing spend divided by the number of leads it produced: CPL = total spend ÷ leads. It's the cleanest way to compare channels at the top of the funnel. To get the full picture, factor in your close rate to reach customer acquisition cost: CAC = CPL ÷ close rate.

How to lower cost per lead

Raise conversion before buying more traffic, shift budget to your lowest-CPL channels, tighten targeting, and improve lead quality so more leads actually close — a cheap lead that never converts is expensive. Fast follow-up is the single biggest lever on close rate.

Frequently asked questions

What is a good cost per lead?
It depends on what a customer is worth. A $100 CPL is great if a customer is worth $2,000 and terrible if they're worth $150. Compare CPL to your average sale and close rate.
CPL vs CAC?
CPL is cost per lead; CAC is cost per closed customer. CAC = CPL ÷ close rate.
How do I calculate cost per lead?
Divide total marketing spend by the number of leads generated in the same period.
