What is the Rule of 40?

The Rule of 40 says a healthy software company's revenue growth rate plus profit margin should be at least 40%. It captures the core trade-off in SaaS: you can grow fast and burn cash, or grow slower and be profitable — but the two together should clear the bar.

How to apply it

Add your year-over-year growth rate to your profit margin (commonly EBITDA or free-cash-flow margin). A company growing 25% with a 20% margin scores 45 and passes; one growing 60% at −25% margin scores 35 and doesn't. Both high-growth and profitable-but-slow companies can clear it in different ways.

Limitations

It's a rule of thumb, not a law. Which margin you use matters, early-stage companies are often judged more on growth, and a single quarter can mislead. Use it as one signal of balance alongside retention, efficiency and the LTV:CAC ratio — not in isolation.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate the Rule of 40?
Add your revenue growth rate to your profit margin. If the total is 40% or more, you pass.
What profit margin should I use?
EBITDA margin or free-cash-flow margin are the most common. Be consistent so the score is comparable over time.
Is the Rule of 40 only for SaaS?
It originated with SaaS but the growth-versus-profitability balance applies to many recurring-revenue businesses.
