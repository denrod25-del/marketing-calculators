What is click-through rate?

CTR is the share of people who click after seeing your ad, email or listing: CTR = clicks ÷ impressions × 100. It's a direct measure of how compelling your creative, offer and targeting are — higher CTR usually means more relevant messaging reaching the right audience.

Typical CTR benchmarks

Benchmarks vary a lot by channel: paid search often runs 2–5%+ on brand and high-intent terms, display is frequently under 0.5%, paid social lands in between, and email click rates commonly sit in the low single digits. Compare against your own history and channel norms rather than a universal number.

Improving CTR

Sharpen the offer and headline, match creative to audience intent, use strong calls to action, and test relentlessly. On search, tight keyword-to-ad relevance lifts CTR and quality score; on social and email, the hook and first line do most of the work.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate CTR?
Divide clicks by impressions and multiply by 100. 2,000 clicks on 100,000 impressions is a 2% CTR.
What is a good CTR?
It depends on channel — paid search can exceed 2–5%, display is often under 0.5%, and email click rates are usually low single digits.
Does CTR affect my ad costs?
Yes. On search platforms a higher CTR improves quality score, which can lower your cost per click.
