How PPC budgeting works

Your budget buys clicks, clicks convert to leads, and leads close into customers: clicks = budget ÷ CPC, leads = clicks × landing-page conversion, customers = leads × close rate. Working backward, the budget for a lead goal is (leads ÷ conversion) × CPC.

How to lower cost per lead

Raise your landing-page conversion before buying more clicks — going from 4% to 8% halves your cost per lead. Tighten keyword match and negatives, improve Quality Score, and make sure every lead gets a fast follow-up so more of them close.

Frequently asked questions

How much should I budget for Google Ads?
Start from your lead goal: leads ÷ landing-page conversion = clicks, then clicks × CPC = budget. Use the Goal → budget mode above.
What is a good PPC conversion rate?
Landing-page conversion of 5%–12% is typical; high-intent search with a focused page can exceed that.
Why is my cost per click so high?
Competitive industries like legal, home services, and dental have CPCs of $8–$10+. Better Quality Score and tighter targeting reduce it.
