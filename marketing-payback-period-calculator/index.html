What is CAC payback period?

Payback period is how many months of gross profit from a customer it takes to earn back what you spent acquiring them: payback = CAC ÷ (monthly revenue × gross margin). It's a cash-flow reality check — the faster you recoup CAC, the sooner that customer funds the next one.

What's a healthy payback?

For recurring-revenue businesses, under 12 months is generally healthy and under 6 is excellent; longer paybacks tie up cash and raise risk. For one-off sales, you want to recover CAC on the first purchase. Faster payback lets you reinvest in growth sooner.

How to shorten payback

Lower CAC (better conversion, cheaper channels, referrals), raise average revenue per customer (upsells, higher tiers), or improve margin. Even small gains compound, because a shorter payback frees cash to acquire more customers faster.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate marketing payback period?
Divide CAC by monthly gross profit per customer (monthly revenue times gross margin). The result is the number of months to recover CAC.
What is a good CAC payback period?
For subscription businesses, under 12 months is healthy and under 6 is excellent. One-off sales should recover CAC on the first purchase.
Why does payback period matter?
It's a cash-flow measure — faster payback means each customer sooner funds acquiring the next, reducing risk and fueling growth.
