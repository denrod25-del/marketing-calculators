How much do display ads cost?

Display and banner ads are cheap on a CPM basis: standard banners run about $1.50–$4 CPM, the Google Display Network averages ~$3.12, native ads $5–$12, and private-marketplace deals ~$8. A reasonable all-in benchmark including data and fees is $4–$10 CPM. Click-through rates are low (often 0.1%–0.5%), so display shines at awareness and retargeting more than direct response.

Where display works best

Use display for cheap reach and, especially, retargeting — showing ads to people who already visited your site converts far better than cold banners and is some of the highest-ROI spend available. Keep creative simple, target tightly, and measure view-through conversions, not just clicks.

Frequently asked questions

How much do display ads cost?
About $1.50–$4 CPM for standard banners, up to $8–$25 for native or premium inventory. An all-in benchmark is $4–$10 CPM.
Why is my display click rate so low?
Display CTR is naturally low (0.1%–0.5%). It's a reach and retargeting channel; judge it on view-through conversions, not clicks.
Is retargeting worth it?
Usually yes — showing display ads to past site visitors is cheap and converts far better than cold banner impressions.
