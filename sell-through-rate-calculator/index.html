What is sell-through rate?

Sell-through rate is the percentage of received inventory sold in a period: units sold ÷ units received × 100. It's usually measured monthly and tells you how quickly stock is moving — high sell-through means demand is strong and inventory is efficient; low means product is sitting.

What's a good sell-through rate?

A commonly cited healthy benchmark is around 40–80% per month, but it varies widely by category, price point and season. Very high sell-through can even signal you under-ordered and left sales on the table; very low points to overstock, mispricing or weak demand.

Using it

Track sell-through by product and category to guide reordering, markdowns and buying. Fast sellers deserve restocks and more prominence; slow movers may need promotion or clearance to free up cash and shelf space. Pair it with inventory turnover for a fuller efficiency picture.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate sell-through rate?
Divide units sold by units received and multiply by 100. 350 sold of 500 received is a 70% sell-through rate.
What is a good sell-through rate?
Roughly 40–80% per month is often considered healthy, but it depends heavily on category, price and season.
What does a low sell-through rate mean?
Inventory is moving slowly — a sign of overstock, mispricing or weak demand that may call for promotion or markdown.
