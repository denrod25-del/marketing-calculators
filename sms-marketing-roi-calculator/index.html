How much does SMS marketing cost?

Text messages cost about $0.01–$0.05 each in the U.S. (MMS with images a bit more), plus a platform fee. Because sends are cheap and texts get read almost immediately, SMS posts some of the highest returns in marketing — industry benchmarks put ROI anywhere from $16 to $70+ per dollar spent, with click-through rates far above email.

Keep SMS compliant and effective

Get explicit opt-in (TCPA), include an easy opt-out, and don't over-send — SMS is a permission channel and overuse burns the list fast. Use it for time-sensitive value: appointment reminders, review requests, limited offers, and reactivation. Personalize and keep it short.

Frequently asked questions

How much does it cost to send a text campaign?
Roughly $0.01–$0.05 per message plus a platform fee. A 3,000-subscriber send at $0.03 is about $90.
What is a good SMS ROI?
SMS commonly returns $16–$70 per dollar spent thanks to near-100% open rates and high click-through.
Is SMS marketing legal?
Yes, with proper opt-in consent and an easy opt-out per TCPA rules. Never text purchased lists.
