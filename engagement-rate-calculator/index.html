What is engagement rate?

Engagement rate measures how much your audience interacts with your content. The common formula is engagements ÷ followers × 100, counting likes, comments, shares and saves. Measuring against reach instead of followers shows how the people who actually saw a post responded — often a truer signal.

How to raise engagement

Post content worth reacting to — ask questions, tell stories, show before/after results, and reply to comments quickly. Saves and shares signal the most value to the algorithm. Consistency and timing matter, but relevance to your specific audience matters most.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate engagement rate?
Divide total engagements (likes, comments, shares, saves) by followers and multiply by 100. Use reach instead of followers for engagement by reach.
What is a good engagement rate?
Roughly 1%–3% is good, 3%–6% great, and 6%+ excellent by followers. Smaller accounts typically run higher.
Should I measure by followers or reach?
Both are useful. By-reach shows how people who actually saw the post responded, which is often a truer signal.
