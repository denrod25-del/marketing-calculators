How website ad revenue works

Display revenue is driven by impressions and CPM: revenue = impressions ÷ 1,000 × CPM. Impressions depend on your pageviews, how many ad units each page shows, and your fill rate (the share of ad slots that actually get sold). More quality pageviews and higher CPMs both lift earnings.

CPM vs RPM

CPM is revenue per 1,000 impressions; RPM is revenue per 1,000 pageviews — the number publishers watch, because it captures ads-per-page and fill rate in one figure. This tool shows both so you can compare against ad-network reports.

What drives higher CPMs

Niche, audience geography, seasonality, viewability and advertiser demand all move CPMs. Finance, tech and B2B audiences command more than general content; tier-1 country traffic earns more than global. Improving viewability and site speed, and adding demand partners, are the most reliable levers.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate ad revenue?
Multiply impressions by CPM and divide by 1,000. Impressions equal pageviews times ad units per page times fill rate.
What is page RPM?
Revenue per 1,000 pageviews. It rolls ads-per-page and fill rate into a single number publishers use to compare pages and sites.
What CPM should I expect?
It varies widely by niche and audience — general content may see low single-digit CPMs while finance, tech and B2B can be much higher.
