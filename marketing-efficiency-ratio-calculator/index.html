What is MER?

Marketing Efficiency Ratio (MER), also called blended ROAS, is total revenue ÷ total marketing spend across all channels. Unlike platform ROAS — which each ad network reports for itself and often double-counts — MER is a single, honest, business-level number that can't be inflated by attribution games.

Why marketers rely on MER

Individual channel ROAS figures overlap (a customer sees an Instagram ad, a Google ad, and an email before buying). MER sidesteps that by dividing all revenue by all spend. It's the cleanest top-line gauge of whether your marketing, as a whole, is efficient — and how a spend increase moves the business.

Reading your MER

A 4:1 MER means $4 of revenue per marketing dollar. What's "good" depends on margin — compare MER to your break-even (1 ÷ margin) just like ROAS. Track the trend: if you scale spend and MER holds, you're growing efficiently; if MER falls fast, you're hitting diminishing returns.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate marketing efficiency ratio?
Divide total revenue by total marketing spend across all channels. A $120,000 revenue on $30,000 spend is a 4:1 MER.
What is the difference between MER and ROAS?
ROAS is usually per-channel and can double-count conversions; MER is blended across all revenue and spend, giving one honest business-level number.
What is a good MER?
It depends on margin — compare it to your break-even (1 divided by gross margin) and watch the trend as you scale spend.
