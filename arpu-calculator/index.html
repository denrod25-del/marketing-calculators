What is ARPU?

Average Revenue Per User is exactly that: total revenue ÷ active users over a period. It's a core metric for subscription, app and media businesses because it shows how much value you extract per user — and how that changes as you add features, raise prices or shift your mix.

ARPU vs ARPPU

ARPU counts all users; ARPPU (average revenue per paying user) counts only those who pay. In freemium models ARPPU is much higher than ARPU, and the gap tells you about conversion. Enter paying users above to see both and your paying share.

Growing ARPU

Lift ARPU through upsells and higher tiers, add-ons, reducing discounting, and improving free-to-paid conversion. Pair ARPU with retention and acquisition cost: rising ARPU with stable churn is one of the cleanest signals of a healthy, scalable business.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate ARPU?
Divide total revenue for a period by the number of active users in that period.
What's the difference between ARPU and ARPPU?
ARPU divides revenue by all users; ARPPU divides by only paying users. ARPPU is higher, especially in freemium models.
Should ARPU be monthly or annual?
Either — just be consistent. This tool shows both the period ARPU and an annualized figure.
