Reach vs frequency

In media planning, reach is how many unique people see your ad and frequency is how many times each sees it. They're linked by impressions = reach × frequency. A fixed budget forces a trade-off: wide reach with low frequency, or narrower reach with higher frequency.

How much frequency do you need?

One exposure rarely sticks. A common rule of thumb is that people need to see a message at least 3 times before it registers — the "effective frequency" idea. For a new or complex offer, aim higher; for a familiar brand or simple reminder, lower frequency can work.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between reach and frequency?
Reach is the number of unique people who see your ad; frequency is how many times each person sees it. Impressions equal reach times frequency.
What is a good ad frequency?
Often at least 3 exposures to register a message. New or complex offers need more; familiar reminders need less.
How do I calculate impressions?
Multiply unique reach by average frequency. This tool also solves for reach or frequency if you know the other two.
