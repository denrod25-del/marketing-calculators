How to calculate marketing ROI

Marketing ROI is the return you earn for every dollar spent: ROI = (revenue − cost) ÷ cost × 100. An ROI of 100% means you doubled your money; 0% means you broke even. A closely related metric, ROAS (return on ad spend) = revenue ÷ cost, is expressed as a ratio like 4:1.

ROI vs ROAS — what's the difference?

ROAS measures gross revenue per ad dollar and is common in paid media. ROI subtracts the cost, so it reflects actual profit contribution. A 4:1 ROAS equals a 300% ROI. Use ROAS to optimize campaigns and ROI to justify budget.

How the funnel mode works

Funnel mode estimates revenue from your pipeline: leads × close rate = customers, customers × average sale = revenue. It then returns ROI, cost per lead and customer acquisition cost (CAC) so you can see the full economics of a campaign.

Frequently asked questions

What is a good marketing ROI?
A common rule of thumb is 5:1 revenue-to-spend (about 400% ROI). Below 2:1 usually loses money once margins and overhead are counted.
How do I calculate ROAS from ROI?
ROAS = (ROI% ÷ 100) + 1. So 300% ROI equals a 4:1 ROAS.
Should I use revenue or profit in ROI?
For a truer picture, use gross profit (revenue × margin) instead of revenue. This calculator uses revenue by default — adjust the average sale to your margin if you want profit-based ROI.
