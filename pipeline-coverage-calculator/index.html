What is pipeline coverage?

Pipeline coverage is the ratio of open pipeline value to your quota: coverage = pipeline ÷ quota. It answers a simple question — given how often you win, do you have enough deals in play to hit your number? A 3x coverage means you have three dollars of pipeline for every dollar of target.

How much coverage do you need?

The target coverage is roughly 1 ÷ win rate. At a 25% win rate you need about 4x pipeline to expect to hit quota; at 33% you need about 3x. This tool computes your target coverage from your win rate and shows whether your current pipeline clears it.

Closing the gap

If you're short, you either need more pipeline (prospecting, campaigns, partnerships) or a higher win rate (better qualification, tighter ICP, stronger sales process). The "pipeline needed" and "gap to fill" figures tell you exactly how much more open opportunity to generate.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate pipeline coverage?
Divide your total open pipeline value by your sales quota for the period. Pipeline of $1.5M against a $500K quota is 3x coverage.
What is a good pipeline coverage ratio?
Roughly 1 divided by your win rate — about 3x–4x for most teams. Lower win rates need more coverage.
What does expected-to-close mean?
Your pipeline value multiplied by your historical win rate — a realistic estimate of revenue those open deals will produce.
