Calculate follower growth rate

Growth rate = net new followers ÷ starting followers × 100.

What is follower growth rate?

Follower growth rate is the net change in your social audience relative to where you started: (followers gained − followers lost) ÷ starting followers × 100. Because it's a percentage, it lets you compare growth fairly across accounts of very different sizes — a 5% month on a large account is a lot of people.

Net growth beats gross follows

Gross new follows look flattering, but unfollows are always happening. Net growth rate is the honest number. A consistently positive rate means your content and posting cadence are attracting more than they lose; a flat or negative rate means it's time to rethink content or targeting.

Growing the right way

Chase engaged followers, not vanity numbers — a smaller, active audience converts better than a large, indifferent one. Post consistently, lean into what earns saves and shares, and avoid follow-for-follow or bought followers that inflate the count but kill engagement rate.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate follower growth rate?
Subtract followers lost from followers gained, divide by your starting follower count, and multiply by 100.
Why use a percentage instead of raw follows?
A percentage lets you compare growth fairly across accounts of different sizes and track momentum over time.
What is a good follower growth rate?
Any consistent positive net rate is healthy. Prioritize engaged followers over raw count, since engagement drives results.
