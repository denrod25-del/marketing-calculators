What is email open rate?

Open rate is the share of delivered emails that were opened: opens ÷ delivered × 100. It reflects how well your subject line, sender name and send timing earn attention in a crowded inbox. Note that privacy features like Apple Mail Privacy Protection can inflate opens, so treat the number as directional.

Open rate, CTR and CTOR

Three metrics tell the story: open rate (attention), click rate / CTR (clicks ÷ delivered — overall engagement), and click-to-open rate / CTOR (clicks ÷ opens — how compelling the content was to those who opened). CTOR is the cleanest read on whether your email body and offer landed.

Typical benchmarks

Averages vary by industry but many lists see open rates in the high teens to 25%+ and click rates in the low single digits. Compare to your own history rather than a universal figure. Improve opens with subject-line testing and list hygiene; improve clicks with clearer offers and calls to action.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate email open rate?
Divide unique opens by emails delivered and multiply by 100.
What is click-to-open rate?
Clicks divided by opens — it measures how engaging your email content was for the people who actually opened it.
Why might my open rate be inflated?
Privacy features like Apple Mail Privacy Protection auto-load images and can register opens that didn't happen, so treat open rate as directional.
