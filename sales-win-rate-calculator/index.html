What is sales win rate?

Win rate is the share of opportunities you close: win rate = deals won ÷ total opportunities × 100. It's the core efficiency metric of your sales process — and it lets you work backward from a revenue goal to the pipeline you need to build.

Planning pipeline from a goal

To hit a revenue target: deals needed = goal ÷ average deal, and opportunities needed = deals needed ÷ win rate. If you close 25% and need 56 deals, you must generate about 224 opportunities. That tells your marketing exactly how many leads to deliver.

How to improve win rate

Qualify harder so you spend time on real buyers, respond fast, follow a consistent process, and address objections early. A higher win rate means you need less pipeline (and less ad spend) to hit the same number — often cheaper than generating more leads.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate win rate?
Divide deals won by total opportunities, then multiply by 100.
What is a good sales win rate?
It varies by industry and deal size; track your own trend. Higher-intent leads and faster follow-up lift it.
How much pipeline do I need?
Divide your revenue goal by average deal value for deals needed, then divide by your win rate for opportunities needed.
