What is CPM?

CPM (cost per mille) is the price of 1,000 ad impressions. It's the standard way to compare the cost of awareness across channels. The formula is CPM = (cost ÷ impressions) × 1,000. Rearranged: impressions = (cost ÷ CPM) × 1,000, and cost = (CPM × impressions) ÷ 1,000.

CPM vs CPC vs CPA

CPM charges for views, CPC for clicks, and CPA for a completed action (a lead or sale). Awareness campaigns are usually bought on CPM; direct-response campaigns care more about CPC and CPA. A low CPM isn't automatically better — cheap impressions that don't convert cost more per customer than pricier, higher-intent ones.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate CPM?
Divide total cost by impressions, then multiply by 1,000. For example, $5,000 for 500,000 impressions is a $10 CPM.
What is a good CPM?
It depends on channel and targeting. Display and static out-of-home can be $2–$10; TV and tightly targeted social run higher. Compare CPM only within the same channel and audience.
How many impressions will my budget buy?
Impressions = (budget ÷ CPM) × 1,000. A $5,000 budget at a $12 CPM buys about 417,000 impressions.
