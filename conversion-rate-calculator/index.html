How to calculate conversion rate

Conversion rate is conversions ÷ visitors × 100. If 120 of 4,000 visitors convert, that's a 3% conversion rate. It applies to any step in the funnel — ad clicks to leads, leads to booked jobs, quotes to closes — so track it at each stage to find your weakest link.

Why small gains matter

Doubling your conversion rate doubles your leads without spending a dollar more on traffic — which is why conversion optimization is usually cheaper than buying more clicks. Clear calls to action, fast load times, mobile-friendly forms, and social proof are the biggest levers.

Frequently asked questions

What is a good conversion rate?
For websites, 2%–5% is average and 8%+ is strong; high-intent paid landing pages can run higher. Compare within your own funnel over time.
How do I calculate traffic needed for a goal?
Divide your conversion goal by your conversion rate. To get 200 conversions at 3%, you need about 6,667 visitors.
How do I improve conversion rate?
Sharpen the offer and CTA, cut form friction, speed up the page, add reviews, and test one change at a time.
