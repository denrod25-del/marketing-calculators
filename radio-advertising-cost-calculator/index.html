How much does radio advertising cost?

A 30-second radio spot runs from about $100 in a small market to $1,400+ in New York or LA, with medium markets in the $200–$800 range. Radio is bought by the spot, and rates rise for drive-time (morning/evening commute) slots. On a per-impression basis, radio CPM is roughly $5–$15.

Getting the most from radio

Frequency matters more than reach — listeners need to hear a spot several times before it registers, so a concentrated schedule on one or two stations usually beats spreading spots thin. Drive-time costs more but delivers the biggest audience. Streaming audio (Spotify, iHeart, Pandora) adds geo and demo targeting radio can't.

Frequently asked questions

How much does a radio ad cost?
Roughly $100–$1,400+ per 30-second spot depending on market and time slot. Small markets are far cheaper than NYC or LA.
What is radio CPM?
About $5–$15 per 1,000 listeners, one of the lower CPMs among traditional media.
Is radio still effective?
Yes, especially local and drive-time. Pair it with streaming audio for targeting and use a memorable phone number or promo code to track response.
