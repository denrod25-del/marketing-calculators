What is break-even ROAS?

Break-even ROAS is the return on ad spend at which your ads exactly pay for themselves after product/delivery costs. Because only your gross margin is available to cover ad cost, the formula is simply break-even ROAS = 1 ÷ gross margin. At a 50% margin you need a 2:1 ROAS to break even; at 25% margin you need 4:1.

Target ROAS for a profit

To earn a profit, you need to beat break-even. For a target profit margin on ad-driven revenue, target ROAS = 1 ÷ (gross margin − target margin). Anything above break-even ROAS is profit; below it, you lose money on every sale.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate break-even ROAS?
Divide 1 by your gross margin expressed as a decimal. A 40% margin means 1 ÷ 0.40 = 2.5:1 break-even ROAS.
Is a 2:1 ROAS good?
Only if your gross margin is 50% or higher. At lower margins, 2:1 loses money. Always compare ROAS to your break-even, not a generic number.
ROAS vs ROI?
ROAS is revenue per ad dollar (a ratio); ROI subtracts cost and shows profit as a percentage. Break-even ROAS translates margin into the ROAS target you must clear.
