How much does a local TV commercial cost?

A 30-second local TV spot runs from about $200–$1,500 in small and mid markets up to $5,000–$50,000+ in top-10 metros. Rates depend on the show, daypart, and audience size. On a per-impression basis, local TV CPM is typically $15–$35. Production is a separate one-time cost.

Broadcast vs cable vs streaming

Cable spots zoned to your county/DMA are cheaper than broadcast and better for local targeting. For even tighter targeting and lower minimums, connected TV (CTV/OTT) lets you reach specific ZIPs and households — often a better fit for a local service business than a broad broadcast buy.

Frequently asked questions

How much does a 30-second TV ad cost?
$200–$1,500 in smaller markets and $5,000–$50,000+ in top metros, plus one-time production.
What is local TV CPM?
Usually $15–$35 per 1,000 viewers, higher than radio or out-of-home but with sight, sound and motion.
Is TV or CTV better for local business?
CTV usually — it targets by ZIP and household with lower minimums, so less spend is wasted outside your service area.
