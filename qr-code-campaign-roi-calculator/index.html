Why QR codes matter for ROI

QR codes are the bridge between offline and digital — they make print, signage, packaging and vehicle wraps trackable. Every scan is a measurable action, so you finally know which flyer, mailer or billboard actually drove business. The funnel is simple: pieces × scan rate = scans, scans × conversion = customers.

Typical scan rates

Scan rates vary widely by placement and offer — often 1%–5%, higher when there's a clear reason to scan (a discount, a booking, a giveaway). Put the code where people have time to scan (a mailer, a waiting-room sign, an invoice) and tell them exactly what they'll get.

Get more scans

Pair the code with a strong call to action ("Scan for 15% off"), make the landing page mobile-first and fast, use a trackable short link per placement, and test placement. A QR code on a vehicle wrap or invoice turns everyday touchpoints into measurable lead sources.

Frequently asked questions

How do I measure QR code campaign ROI?
Multiply pieces by scan rate for scans, scans by conversion for customers, and compare revenue to campaign cost.
What is a typical QR scan rate?
Often 1%–5%, higher when there's a clear incentive to scan and the code is placed where people can act.
Where should I use QR codes?
On mailers, flyers, signage, packaging, invoices and vehicle wraps — anywhere you want to make offline marketing trackable.
