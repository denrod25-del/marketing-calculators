What is cost per engagement?

Cost per engagement (CPE) is what you pay for each interaction with your ad — likes, comments, shares, saves, clicks or other engagements: ad spend ÷ total engagements. Some platforms even let you bid on a CPE basis, charging only when someone engages rather than per impression.

CPE alongside engagement rate

CPE tells you cost efficiency; engagement rate (engagements ÷ impressions) tells you how compelling the content is. Together they diagnose performance: a high CPE with a low engagement rate points to weak creative or poor targeting, while a low CPE with a healthy rate means the content is resonating cheaply.

Improving CPE

Better creative is the biggest lever — content people actually want to interact with lowers CPE directly. Sharpen targeting so the right people see it, test hooks and formats, and lean into whatever earns saves and shares. Match the engagement type to your goal so you're not paying for interactions that don't move the business.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate cost per engagement?
Divide total ad spend by the number of engagements. $500 for 2,500 engagements is $0.20 per engagement.
What counts as an engagement?
Depends on the platform — typically likes, comments, shares, saves, clicks and other interactions with your ad or post.
How do I lower CPE?
Improve creative so people want to interact, tighten targeting, and test formats and hooks to find what resonates cheaply.
