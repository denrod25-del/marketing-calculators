What is cost per view?

CPV is what you pay each time someone views your video ad: CPV = spend ÷ views. It's the standard efficiency metric for video, especially YouTube skippable ads where you're only charged when a viewer watches enough of the ad or interacts.

Typical CPV benchmarks

YouTube skippable in-stream CPV commonly runs $0.03–$0.12 per view; social video views can be cheaper or dearer depending on the platform and how a "view" is counted (2 seconds vs. 30 seconds vs. completed). Always check the view definition before comparing platforms.

CPV vs CPM

CPV charges per view; CPM charges per 1,000 impressions whether or not they're watched. For a view-based goal, CPV aligns cost with attention. This tool also shows the equivalent CPM so you can compare a CPV buy against impression-based inventory.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate cost per view?
Divide total ad spend by the number of views. This tool also solves for views or spend if you know the other two.
What is a good cost per view?
YouTube skippable in-stream often runs $0.03–$0.12 per view; it varies by platform, targeting and how a view is defined.
What's the difference between CPV and CPM?
CPV charges per view; CPM charges per 1,000 impressions regardless of whether they're watched.
