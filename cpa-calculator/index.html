What is cost per acquisition?

CPA is what you pay to win one customer: CPA = total spend ÷ number of acquisitions. Unlike cost per lead, it counts only customers who actually convert, so it reflects the true cost of growth. (It's the same idea as customer acquisition cost, or CAC.)

Is your CPA sustainable?

CPA only matters against what a customer is worth. The LTV:CPA ratio is the health check — 3:1 or higher is the widely used target. Below 1:1 you lose money on every customer; far above 5:1 you might be under-spending and leaving growth on the table.

How to lower CPA

Improve conversion before buying more traffic, shift budget to your lowest-CPA channels, tighten targeting, and raise close rate with faster follow-up. Because CPA divides by customers, anything that lifts conversion lowers it directly.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate cost per acquisition?
Divide total marketing spend by the number of new customers acquired in the same period.
What is a good CPA?
There's no universal figure — compare it to lifetime value. Aim for an LTV:CPA ratio of at least 3:1.
Is CPA the same as CAC?
Effectively yes — both measure the cost to acquire a customer. CPA is also sometimes used per conversion action.
