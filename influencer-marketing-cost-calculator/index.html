How much does influencer marketing cost?

Rates scale with audience size. In 2026, typical per-post rates run about $50–$300 for nano creators (1K–10K), $200–$2,500 for micro (10K–100K), $2,500–$25,000 for larger creators (100K+), and $10,000+ for mega/celebrity (1M+). Video (Reels, TikTok, YouTube) commands the top of each range.

Why micro and nano often win

Smaller creators usually have higher engagement and much lower rates, so cost per result can beat big names — especially for local and niche audiences. Use unique promo codes or links to track sales, and favor creators whose audience actually matches your service area and customer.

Frequently asked questions

How much do influencers charge per post?
Roughly $50–$300 for nano, $200–$2,500 for micro, thousands for larger creators, and $10,000+ for mega/celebrity. Video costs more.
What is a good influencer marketing ROI?
Industry studies often cite around $5–$6 per $1 spent, but it varies widely; track with unique codes or links.
Are micro-influencers worth it?
Often yes — higher engagement and lower rates can beat big names on cost per result, especially for local or niche audiences.
