How to calculate SEO ROI

SEO ROI is (revenue from organic − SEO cost) ÷ SEO cost. The catch is timing: rankings build over months, so returns are small early and compound later. This tool assumes traffic ramps roughly linearly to your steady-state target, so the modeled visits over the period are about half of full-run-rate — a realistic, conservative view.

Why SEO ROI beats paid over time

Paid traffic stops the moment you stop paying; SEO keeps compounding. Once a page ranks, each additional visit is essentially free, so the cost per lead falls every month while paid stays flat. That's why mature SEO programs often post the highest ROI of any channel — but they need 6–12 months to prove out.

Frequently asked questions

How long until SEO pays off?
Usually 6–12 months to meaningful traffic, depending on competition and site authority. Local SEO can move faster than national.
What's a good SEO ROI?
Mature programs frequently exceed 5:1 revenue-to-cost because ongoing traffic is nearly free once you rank.
How do I measure SEO revenue?
Track organic visits, the share that become leads, and the share of leads that close, then multiply by average sale value.
