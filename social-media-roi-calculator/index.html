How to measure social media ROI

The honest formula is (revenue from social − total social cost) ÷ total social cost. The mistake most businesses make is counting only ad spend and ignoring the biggest real cost — the time spent creating content and managing accounts. Include tools and time and you get a true picture.

Attributing revenue to social

Track social-driven leads with UTM links, dedicated landing pages, promo codes, or a simple "how did you hear about us?" question. Organic social often assists conversions it doesn't get credit for, so also watch assisted conversions, not just last-click.

Improving social ROI

Double down on the format and platform that actually drives leads, repurpose one idea across channels to cut production time, and boost your best organic posts rather than starting cold. Lowering the time cost is often the fastest way to raise ROI.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate social media ROI?
Subtract total social cost (ads, tools and time) from social-driven revenue, then divide by that cost.
Should I include my time in the cost?
Yes — content creation and account management time is usually the largest real cost of social and ignoring it inflates ROI.
How do I attribute revenue to social?
Use UTM links, dedicated landing pages, promo codes, or a how-did-you-hear question, and watch assisted conversions too.
