What is email list growth rate?

It's the net change in your subscriber base over a period, as a percentage of where you started: (new subscribers − unsubscribes and bounces) ÷ starting list × 100. Gross sign-ups look great on their own; the net rate tells you whether the list is truly growing after losses.

Why lists shrink even while adding subscribers

Email lists naturally decay — people unsubscribe, change addresses, and hard-bounce. A commonly cited figure is that lists lose roughly a fifth or more of contacts per year to decay. If your sign-up rate doesn't outpace that churn, your reachable audience is quietly shrinking.

Growing the list healthily

Prioritize quality over raw size: opt-in forms with a clear value exchange, lead magnets, and consistent sending keep engagement high. Regularly clean inactive and bounced addresses — a smaller, engaged list deliverability-wise beats a large, decaying one.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate email list growth rate?
Subtract unsubscribes and bounces from new subscribers, divide by your starting list size, and multiply by 100.
What is a good email list growth rate?
Positive and outpacing natural decay. Because lists lose a meaningful share of contacts yearly, sustained net growth is the goal.
Why is my list shrinking despite new sign-ups?
Email lists decay through unsubscribes, address changes and bounces. If churn exceeds sign-ups, net growth turns negative.
