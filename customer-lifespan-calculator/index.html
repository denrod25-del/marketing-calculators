How customer lifespan is calculated

Average customer lifespan is the inverse of your churn rate: lifespan = 1 ÷ churn rate. A 5% monthly churn means the average customer stays 1 ÷ 0.05 = 20 months. It's a simple but powerful figure because it converts an abstract churn percentage into an intuitive "how long they stick around."

Why it matters for LTV

Lifespan is a core input to lifetime value: multiply average revenue per period by lifespan (and by gross margin) to estimate what a customer is worth. Lowering churn even slightly extends lifespan and compounds directly into higher LTV — which is why retention is so valuable.

A note on the math

The 1 ÷ churn formula assumes a roughly steady churn rate. Real cohorts often churn faster early and stabilize later, so treat this as a clean estimate rather than an exact prediction. Track churn by cohort over time for a more precise picture.
